# fix the KDA (since realisitically the most kills you can get is 30)
def fixDataFrame(df, cname, MAX_KILLS, MAX_ASSISTS):
    df[cname[0]] = [min(MAX_KILLS, x) for x in df[cname[1]]]
    df[cname[1]] = [min(MAX_KILLS + MAX_ASSISTS, x) for x in df[cname[1]]]
    
    # subset only games greater than 5 to avoid outliers
    df = df[df[cname[2]] > 2]
    df = df[df[cname[2]] < 1000]
    
    # eliminate supports and jungler role (will expand on this)
    df = df[df[cname[3]] > 60]
    return df

There doesn't seem to be an easy way to distinguish the two groups from each other so a method such as SVM or KNN would not be viable. These require us to be able to distinguish the location of the data points. SVM draws a boundary and KNN makes decisions based on the neighbors of the points. But since all the points are close together, it's hard to use these algorithms.

from sklearn.tree import DecisionTreeClassifier
import sklearn.linear_model

def plot_decision_surface(clf, X_train, Y_train, title):
    plot_step=0.1
    
    if X_train.shape[1] != 2:
        raise ValueError("X_train should have exactly 2 columnns!")
    
    x_min, x_max = X_train[:, 0].min() - plot_step, X_train[:, 0].max() + plot_step
    y_min, y_max = X_train[:, 1].min() - plot_step, X_train[:, 1].max() + plot_step
    xx, yy = np.meshgrid(np.arange(x_min, x_max, plot_step),
                         np.arange(y_min, y_max, plot_step))

    clf.fit(X_train,Y_train)
    if hasattr(clf, 'predict_proba'):
        Z = clf.predict_proba(np.c_[xx.ravel(), yy.ravel()])[:,1]
    else:
        Z = clf.predict(np.c_[xx.ravel(), yy.ravel()])    
    Z = Z.reshape(xx.shape)
    cs = plt.contourf(xx, yy, Z, cmap=plt.cm.Reds)
    plt.scatter(X_train[:,0],X_train[:,1],c=Y_train,cmap=plt.cm.Paired)
    plt.title(title)
    plt.xlabel("win rate")
    plt.ylabel("kd")
    plt.scatter([], [], color = 'crimson', marker = 'o', label = 'win')
    plt.scatter([], [], color = 'grey', marker = 'o', label = 'loss')
    plt.legend(bbox_to_anchor=(1.12, 1), loc=1, borderaxespad=0.)
    plt.show()
    
carry_sub = carry_df[["win rate", "kd"]]
X_sub = carry_sub.as_matrix()
dTree = DecisionTreeClassifier()
dTree.fit(X_sub, Y)
plot_decision_surface(dTree, X_sub, Y, "Decision Tree Decision Surface")

forest_sub = RandomForestClassifier(n_estimators = 15) 
forest_sub.fit(X_sub, Y)
plot_decision_surface(forest_sub, X_sub, Y, "Random Forest Decision Surface")

svm = sklearn.svm.SVC(C = 100, gamma = 1.0, probability = True)
svm.fit(X_sub, Y)
plot_decision_surface(svm, X_sub, Y, "SVM Decision Surface")

The SVM is not a good predictor. This is because the SVM draws a boundary and there's no distinguishable boundary between the win and loss points. However the Random Forest Decision surface seems to make some intutitive sense. It predicts that players who enter the game with a higher win rate on the champion they are playing are more likely to win. However an interesting pattern to note is that the decision surface is bottom-right. It predicts that players with a lower kda on their champion are more likely to win. This is somewhat contradictory as one would suppose that a player with more kills and less death would tends to win since we believe that kills are positively correlated with chances of winning.

Our data tells another story: it suggests that the game League of Legends a relatively lower kill death ratio improves the chance of winning. Our data tells us that those players who aren't greedy for kills have a better chance of winning, which makes sense since league is a team game that requires cooperation among the 5 members. Our data also tells us that the popularly believed concept that a skilled individual can carry a team is incorrect, which is represented by the cluster of data points with high kda and high winrate which are mostly losses. A team where all players are equally skills, represented by the cluster of wins with moderately high kda and high past win rates, is the most successful.
